Work

Twenty years translating complex problems into things that actually ship — whether that's an enterprise operating model or a Saturday-morning quote that lands in your inbox without anyone touching a keyboard.

I'm Rahul Nair, principal of Cantilever Holdings. Most recently I spent twelve years at Fidelity Investments, the last several as Director of Strategy & Planning for Asset Management Technology — building the portfolio governance, executive reporting, and Agile delivery standards that turned a fragmented technology organization into one with a reliable operating rhythm. Before that, the arc looks less linear than it actually was: I came up as a Cisco solutions architect and enterprise video engineer, standing up the U.S. federal government's nationwide video infrastructure, including the communications backbone for a presidential transition. That consulting-and-systems instinct never left — it's just been pointed at different problems since.

What connects the two halves of that career is a habit of treating organizational change the same way I'd treat a build: form a hypothesis, ship something small, read the actual evidence when it breaks, and let that evidence — not the original plan — decide what happens next. At Fidelity that meant identifying an anti-pattern that reshaped a business unit's resourcing, or running a Pareto analysis that turned a stalled content strategy into an 85% sales win rate. Outside of work it means I'll happily spend a weekend rebuilding a personal automation through four different architectures before landing on the one that actually holds up — and writing up what each failure taught me, because that part is usually more interesting than the part that worked the first time.

Cantilever Holdings is where that instinct now operates full-time: technology strategy, portfolio governance, and change enablement for organizations that need someone equally comfortable in front of a steering committee and inside a terminal.
